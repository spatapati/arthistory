Depiction of religious characters pre-Pestilence and post-Pestilence



Pre-Pestilence: Religious figures are exaggerated

Often put on a higher pedestal.

Big bold halos.

Red colors: symbolize the blood of Christ, and often worn by high ranking clergy.

Blue colors: A royal color made from expensive pigment.

Not relatable.

Lack of authenticity between mother and child (Mary and Jesus).

Often enlarged.

Post-Pestilence: Religious figures are made more relatable.

No longer placed on a higher plane

No longer wear royal clothes and colors.

Chiaroscuro and Sfumato are techniques used to show importance.





Renaissance Techniques

Chiaroscuro: Strong contrast between light and dark. Draws attention to certain elements of the painting (people, objects, etc.).

Sfumato: Absence of bold or rigid borders or outlines. Softness/roundness and illusion of depth.

Perspective: Gives 3D effect the illusion of depth. All lines point to one spot; painting establishes one point furthest away from the viewer (vanishing point) and zooms/ focuses in on it. As the images reach closer to the margins/ end of the page, the sizes of the objects in the image increase (drawn larger to scale), this gives illusion of being closer to the viewer.
